QUESTIONS

Before you get in touch.

Is Acceptor a payment processor?

No. Acceptor is an independent consultancy — we review your setup and refer you to an accredited PayFac or PSP that suits your business. We're never the counterparty on your merchant agreement.

What does it cost me to use Acceptor?

The review is free. If we refer you to a provider and you switch, we may receive a referral fee from that provider — it doesn't change the rate you're quoted.

How long does switching actually take?

Typically around 5 business days from signed application to live terminal, depending on the provider and whether new hardware needs to ship.

What if I'm already happy with my current provider?

Then the free review still tells you whether "happy" also means "well-priced" — worth knowing either way, with no obligation to switch.

Is my statement data safe to share?

Yes — statements are used only to benchmark your rate and are not shared beyond what's needed to prepare provider comparisons.

Which providers does Acceptor work with?

A panel of accredited Australian PayFacs and PSPs. Which one we refer you to depends on your volume, category and risk profile — never a fixed default.

How Acceptor actually works

Acceptor is an independent payments consultancy — not a bank, payment facilitator (PayFac), acquirer or PSP. We review your current setup, then refer you directly to the payment facilitator or PSP best suited to your business, and support the switch. You'll always know exactly who you're signing with before you do — Acceptor is never the counterparty on your merchant agreement.

Rates shown on this page are indicative, based on recent merchant outcomes. Your exact terms, contract and merchant agreement are with the PayFac/PSP you're referred to, not with Acceptor.
